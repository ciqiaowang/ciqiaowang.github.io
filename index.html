Jun Lei (born 16 December 1969) is a Chinese billionaire entrepreneur and philanthropist. He is known for founding the consumer electronics company Xiaomi. As of October 2022, Jun Lei's net worth was estimated at either US$8.1 billion according to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index, making him the 203rd richest person in the world， or at $7.5 billion by Forbes, ranking him 265th worldwide.



Jun Lei is a prominent Chinese entrepreneur and technology industry leader. He is best known as the co-founder and CEO of Xiaomi Corporation, a multinational electronics company based in Beijing, China. Born on December 16, 1969, in Xiantao, Hubei, China, Jun Lei has made significant contributions to the technology sector.

Before founding Xiaomi, Jun Lei had a successful career in the industry. He co-founded Joyo.com, an online bookstore, which was later acquired by Amazon. Following the acquisition, Jun Lei served as the President of Amazon China. However, his entrepreneurial spirit led him to establish Xiaomi in 2010.

Jun Lei's vision for Xiaomi was to create high-quality, affordable smartphones for the Chinese market. Under his leadership, Xiaomi experienced rapid growth and became one of the world's leading smartphone manufacturers. The company expanded its product range to include various consumer electronics and smart home devices.

Often compared to Steve Jobs, Jun Lei is admired for his charismatic leadership style and his focus on product design and user experience. He has received numerous awards and recognition for his contributions to the technology industry. Additionally, Jun Lei is actively involved in philanthropy and has made substantial donations to various charitable causes in China.
